Our Mission

To build treehouses in urban areas that clean the air, lower the local temperature, gather real-time environmental data, and become learning and healing spaces for communities.

Our Approach

Swasia stands at the crossroads of architecture, ecology, and technology — proving that nature doesn't need to be kept outside our cities. Nature is the city.

Community Impact

Each treehouse becomes a gathering place for learning, healing, and environmental awareness, creating lasting connections between people and nature in urban spaces.

The Heart of Swasia: Sia

When we were looking for a name for our daughter, we turned to AI for inspiration. Among the names were Kalki, a symbol of powerful change, and Kayra, a name that brings peace. But one name stood out: Sia — meaning victory, blessing, and grace.

Swasia is her legacy — a project that carries the spirit of her name forward. It's for her, and every child like her, who deserves to grow up in a world where clean air and green space aren't luxuries — they're rights.

"Dedicated to my daughter, Sia — and to every child who deserves to grow up breathing clean air, under the shade of trees."

What We're Building

Each Swasia Treehouse is a living laboratory that combines nature, technology, and community.

Built Around Trees

Each treehouse is built around existing trees or within microforests

Sustainable Materials

Using child-safe and recycled materials for construction

Environmental Sensors

Measuring air quality, cooling effects, and biodiversity indicators

Learning Spaces

Public spaces for environmental education and community gathering

Real-time Dashboard

Community access to live environmental data and impact metrics

Community Hub

Bringing neighborhoods together through shared green spaces

Why It Matters

Cities are getting hotter every year — heatwaves are deadly

Urban green cover is disappearing at an alarming rate

Children spend less time outdoors than prisoners

Air pollution causes millions of premature deaths annually

Swasia's purpose is to bring nature back to where people live — and to show, with data and experience, that this isn't just good for the planet, it's essential for our health, happiness, and future.

Our First Step

Location

Uchana, Haryana, India

The first Swasia Treehouse will be built here, featuring the sacred Triveni:

  • Peepal - The sacred fig tree
  • Bargad - The mighty banyan
  • Neem - The healing tree

This combination provides unmatched shade, oxygen, and healing benefits, creating a space where children can climb, breathe, learn, and dream.

Gathering real-time air and temperature data to demonstrate the measurable benefits of urban nature
